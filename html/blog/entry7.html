Top 5 Activities for Nature Enthusiasts



See our top 5 activities to do

  1. Marino Ballena National Park Tour (Uvita) Explore the famous Marino Ballena National Park, known for its incredible biodiversity and beautiful beaches. The tour includes whale watching (seasonal), snorkeling around the Ballena Island, and guided walks through lush tropical forests, all while learning about marine conservation efforts. You can book a snorkeling or whales and dolphins tour with us. Read more here
  2. Rainforest Hike and Waterfall Visit (Dominical) Take a guided hike through the dense rainforest near Dominical. This tour will bring you to hidden waterfalls and pristine natural pools, perfect for a refreshing swim. Learn about the diverse plant and animal species that call this forest home, including how the local community works to protect this natural treasure. Read here about Nauyaca Waterfalls.
  3. El Pavón Waterfall (Vergel) El Pavón Waterfall is located just 5 minutes from the reserve. Visit one of the most incredible waterfalls in Costa Rica. And enjoy a delicious fried tilapia at Tilapias el Pavón afterwards. See Location
  4. Wildlife and Bird Watching Tour (Uvita) Uvita is a prime spots for birdwatching, home to species such as toucans, scarlet macaws, manakins, tanagers, anteaters, and fiery-billed aracari. Guided tours provide the best opportunities for bird sightings and give insight into the local flora and fauna, as well as conservation challenges.
  5. Corcovado National Park Day Trip (Accessible from Uvita & Dominical) A day trip to Corcovado National Park, one of the most biodiverse places on the planet. Explore the trails with an expert guide and discover rare wildlife, including monkeys, jaguars, and countless bird species, while learning about ongoing efforts to preserve this remarkable ecosystem. Book it here

Whether you are interested in hiking through tropical forests, exploring remote waterfalls, or observing the region’s incredible wildlife, these eco-tours offer the perfect opportunity to connect with nature while supporting local conservation initiatives. Join a guided tour, and contribute to the sustainable tourism movement in Costa Rica!
