Visiting Costa Rica in the Rainy Season



Visiting Costa Rica in the Green Season

Get tips for exploring Costa Rica during its lush, rainy season.

The Green Season, also known as the rainy season, runs from May to November in Costa Rica. While this time of year brings frequent rain, it also offers unique opportunities to explore the country's natural beauty and abundant wildlife. Here are some tips for making the most of your visit during the Green Season:

1. Embrace the Rain

The rain is a part of Costa Rica's charm during the Green Season. Pack lightweight, quick-drying clothing and a good waterproof jacket. The rain often falls in short bursts during the afternoon or evening, leaving the mornings free for outdoor activities.

2. Explore the Lush Rainforests

The Green Season is when the country's rainforests are at their most vibrant. You'll see an explosion of green, with lush foliage and thriving wildlife. Areas like the Monteverde Cloud Forest or Corcovado National Park offer a perfect blend of lush scenery and wildlife spotting.

3. Fewer Crowds and Lower Prices

The Green Season brings fewer tourists, which means you'll have more peaceful experiences at popular attractions like Arenal Volcano and the beaches of the Pacific Coast. Many hotels and tours also offer discounts during this time, making it an affordable way to explore Costa Rica.

4. Prepare for Muddy Trails

The rain can make hiking trails slippery and muddy, so wear sturdy, waterproof footwear. If you're hiking in areas like the Osa Peninsula or around Arenal, bring extra socks and prepare to get a little muddy!

5. Don't Miss the Waterfalls

The rain feeds Costa Rica’s many waterfalls, making them even more spectacular during the Green Season. Popular falls like La Fortuna and Río Celeste are particularly stunning after rainfall, with powerful flows and vibrant blue waters.

6. Enjoy the Lush Beaches

While the Green Season might bring some rain to the beach towns, the tropical showers often don’t last long. Coastal areas like the Nicoya Peninsula or the South Pacific region, including Dominical and Uvita, offer a more relaxed and serene atmosphere during this time.

7. Stay Safe and Be Flexible

Weather conditions can change quickly in the Green Season. Always check weather forecasts before heading out and be prepared for sudden changes. Flexibility in your itinerary will help you make the most of each day, adjusting plans as needed due to rain or other weather conditions.

With the right preparation and a sense of adventure, the Green Season can be one of the best times to explore Costa Rica’s vibrant landscapes and wildlife. Enjoy the lush, rain-soaked beauty of the country while avoiding the crowds of the high season!
