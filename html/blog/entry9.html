Costa Rican Cuisine



Local Flavors of Costa Rica

When you come to Costa Rica these are some dishes you cannot miss.

Costa Rican cuisine offers a rich variety of dishes influenced by its indigenous heritage, as well as Spanish and Afro-Caribbean flavors. While gallo pinto is a classic favorite, there is much more to explore. Here are some of the delicious dishes you should try:

1. Casado

A staple meal consisting of rice, beans, salad, fried plantains, and a choice of protein, such as chicken, beef, or fish. It's the perfect example of a well-balanced Costa Rican meal. Curiously the name casado (married) is because in older times when men would go to work in the fields the ones' married would have better lunches compared to their single coworkers. Learn more here.

2. Ceviche

A refreshing dish made with fresh fish or shrimp, marinated in lime juice with cilantro, onions, and peppers. This dish is perfect for a warm day and showcases Costa Rica's coastal flavors. Learn more

3. Olla de Carne

A hearty beef and vegetable stew, typically made with potatoes, corn, carrots, and yucca. It's a traditional dish that reflects the country’s agricultural richness. Read the recipe

4. Sopa Negra

A black bean soup that is a comforting and nutritious dish, often served with a hard-boiled egg and rice. It's simple yet flavorful. The beans are much needed in the Costa Rican society. Learn more

5. Tamal

A typical holiday dish made of masa (corn dough) filled with meat, vegetables, and sometimes rice, wrapped in banana leaves and steamed. It's especially popular during Christmas.Tamales are a typical dish

6. Pico de Gallo

A fresh salsa made of chopped tomatoes, onions, cilantro, and chili, usually served alongside meats, beans, or tortillas. It's a perfect accompaniment for almost any dish. Preparation

7. Arroz con Leche

A sweet dessert made of rice, milk, sugar, and cinnamon. It's a comforting, dessert-like version of rice pudding. Learn how to prepate it

By diving into the local flavors of Costa Rica, you can discover the diversity and richness of its cuisine, which reflects the country’s culture and natural bounty. Whether you’re in the mountains or by the coast, there’s always something new and delicious to savor!
