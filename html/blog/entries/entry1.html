The Osa Peninsula



Nestled in the South Pacific region of Costa Rica, the Osa Peninsula is a sanctuary of unparalleled natural beauty. Known as one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth, this lush corner of the world covers less than 0.001% of the planet’s surface yet harbors an astonishing 2.5% of all species globally. From its pristine rainforests to its vibrant coastal ecosystems, the Osa Peninsula is a haven for nature enthusiasts, scientists, and conservationists alike.

At Ecos del Bosque, we are privileged to be part of this incredible ecosystem. Our wildlife reserve, eco-lodge, and sustainable farming project are deeply rooted in our mission to preserve and celebrate the wonders of the Osa Peninsula while offering visitors an unforgettable connection to nature.

What Makes the Osa Peninsula So Unique?

The Osa Peninsula’s significance lies in its incredible diversity of ecosystems. It is home to dense rainforests, winding mangroves, sprawling beaches, and nutrient-rich marine habitats. These distinct environments support a dazzling variety of plant and animal life, many of which are found nowhere else on Earth.

One of the crown jewels of the peninsula is Corcovado National Park, often referred to as the "crown jewel" of Costa Rica’s national parks. Corcovado boasts primary rainforests, vibrant wetlands, and an unmatched density of wildlife.

Wildlife You’ll Encounter

Visitors to the Osa Peninsula are often amazed by the sheer abundance of wildlife, both large and small.

  • Scarlet Macaws: These iconic birds, with their vivid red, yellow, and blue plumage, are a symbol of the region. Their raucous calls are a constant reminder of the vibrancy of the jungle.
  • Baird’s Tapir: Known as a "living fossil," this gentle herbivore plays a crucial role in maintaining the health of the rainforest. Spotting one is a rare and magical experience.
  • Squirrel Monkeys: The playful squirrel monkeys swing through the trees in troops, delighting visitors with their antics.
  • Jaguar and Puma: While elusive, these apex predators are a vital part of the ecosystem, symbolizing the wild, untamed spirit of the peninsula.
  • Marine Life: The waters surrounding the peninsula are no less impressive, hosting dolphins, humpback whales, and vibrant coral reefs teeming with fish.

A Sanctuary Under Threat

The Osa Peninsula's extraordinary biodiversity is under constant threat from deforestation, climate change, and human encroachment. Recognizing the need for urgent action, conservation organizations and local initiatives work tirelessly to protect the area.

At Ecos del Bosque, we contribute by maintaining our reserve as a safe haven for wildlife, minimizing our ecological footprint, and educating our visitors on sustainable practices. Every guest who visits plays a role in supporting these efforts.

Your Role in Conservation

Visiting the Osa Peninsula isn’t just about enjoying its natural beauty—it’s about participating in a larger movement to protect one of the last great wild places on Earth.

When you stay at Ecos del Bosque, your visit supports:

  • Reforestation projects to expand wildlife habitats.
  • Educational programs for local communities and visitors to foster environmental awareness.
  • Sustainable tourism practices that ensure the longevity of this precious ecosystem.

We offer a variety of experiences designed to deepen your connection to the peninsula’s incredible biodiversity. From guided wildlife tours and birdwatching expeditions to treks through our forest trails, each activity helps you appreciate the Osa Peninsula’s natural wonders while contributing to its preservation.

Plan Your Visit

The best way to experience the magic of the Osa Peninsula is to see it for yourself. Whether you're a seasoned naturalist or a curious traveler, Ecos del Bosque offers the perfect gateway to explore this breathtaking region.

Stay in our eco-lodge, where comfort meets sustainability, and wake up to the calls of howler monkeys and the chirping of exotic birds. Spend your days discovering the secrets of the rainforest, learning about sustainable farming, or simply soaking in the tranquility of untouched nature.

By choosing to visit the Osa Peninsula, you are not just embarking on an unforgettable journey—you are helping to ensure its protection for generations to come.

Let’s work together to preserve the Osa Peninsula and all its wonders. Book your stay at Ecos del Bosque and become a part of this extraordinary story.
